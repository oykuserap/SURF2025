On June 1  , Chairman Kalkanli attended and spoke at the Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, and  
Accessibility (DEIA) Summit hosted by the North Texas Disability Chamber. Chairman  
Kalkanli emphasized the importance of intersectional disability conversations and

Chairman Kalkanli shared that July is Disability Pride Month, with July 26   being the 34    
anniversary of the Americans with Disabilities Act. Chairman Kalkanli suggested that the  
Commission on Disabilities host a public ADA Anniversary Celebration at City Hall in  
place of a formal business July 2024 meeting. Chairman Kalkanli opened the floor for  
discussion.   

Commissioner Puga expressed favor for the idea but expressed concern regarding the  
logistics of the event and requested additional details. Commissioner Puga emphasized  
the importance of the public and the Dallas community being a part of such a celebration.   

Attorney Veronica Barboza, City Attorney’s Office, provided advice regarding the logistics  
of an event supported by the Commission and Texas Open Meetings Act (TOMA)  
guidelines.  

Chris Graves, Human Rights Officer, provided the recommendation for the Commission to  
vote on this motion to ensure proper protocol and how the Office of Equity and Inclusion  
would be able to support the Commission in this effort.   

Commissioner Bellew expressed favor for the idea but expressed concern regarding the  
timeline for pulling an event together.   

Chairman Kalkanli clarified his suggestion and answered the Commissioner’s questions.   

Commissioner White expressed that she would attend the event if the motion passes.  

 Chairman Kalkanli reaffirmed the Commission’s aim to safeguard the dignity and rights of  
residents living with disabilities and his hope for the event.  

Commissioner Revi expressed that she would not be able to attend the event, due to a  
previous conflict.   

Commissioner Bellew called for a vote on the motion. The motion passed with all in favor.

COMMISSIONERS’ REPORTS  

Commissioner Puga shared the following Commissioner’s Report:  

On June 5th , 2024, Commissioner Puga attended the 2024 Pride in Excellence Awards,  

hosted by the Mayor and City Council, where equity, inclusion, justice, and human rights  
were discussed. Mayor Pro Tem Atkins and the majority of the Dallas City Council read  
the proclamation for Pride Month. Commissioner Puga mentioned Councilmember Chad  
West District 1 and Former Mayor Pro Tem Adam Medrano speaking, and State Rep  
Venton Jones being in attendance.   

On June 11th , 2024, District 3 (Councilmember Zarin D. Gracey) held a ribbon cutting for  

their grocery store Joe V’s Smart Shop. Commissioner Puga referenced the importance of  
this grocery store being in South Dallas, a food desert. Commissioner Puga mentioned  
that disabilities, accessibility, and mobility to shop were discussed at the ribbon cutting.  
Commissioner Puga mentioned and thanked the following individuals in attendance:  
Mayor Pro Tem Tennell Atkins, Pastor Rush of the Inspiring Body of Christ, Deputy Mayor  
Pro Tem Adam Bazaldua, Sheriff Marian Brown, Sergeant Prentice Richmond, and all  
other City of Dallas and Dallas County officials.   

On June 12th , 2024, the Disability Regional Chamber (DRC) Inclusion Conference was  

held at UNT Dallas, sponsored in part by Invited Clubs. Cynt Marshall, CEO of the Dallas  
Mavericks, Mayor Rachel Proctor, City of Desoto, and Dylan Rafaty, CEO of North Texas  
Disability Chamber were in attendance. Breakout sessions included:   

    •   Advancing DEI Using an Organizational Maturity Model  

    •   The Importance of Wellness and Mental Health in the Workplace  

    •   Leveraging Talent: The Competitive Advantage of Disability Inclusion  

    •   Empowering Community Engagement Through Corporate Action  

  On June 18th , 2024, the City of Dallas hosted Juneteenth: Commemorate, Educate, and  

  Celebrate at City Hall. City Council members were in attendance and Councilmember  
  Zarin D. Gracey read the proclamation. Coach Greg Ellis, former Dallas Cowboy was  
  awarded a street sign in his name, to acknowledge his leadership and continued  
  community support and activism, relating to Juneteenth and African American History  
  and Culture.   

  Commissioner Puga met with Robert Stowe, Vice President of Texas Region/Convention  
  Center Executive at McKissack and McKissack to discuss disability, accessibility, and the  
  importance of inclusive construction plans. Commissioner Puga emphasized the  
  importance of these discussions continuing in construction, especially in schools.

  Commissioner Revi provided the following report:  

  Commissioner Revi was contacted by a member of the public who asked where she  
  could find a carpenter who could build a ramp for her house. Commissioner Revi  
  reported connecting the resident with the Texas Ramp.   

  Commissioner Revi met with the regional director of Cruise. Commissioner Revi reports  
  that these vehicles are not ADA-compliant and do not provide a means of access if  
  someone uses a wheelchair. Commissioner Revi mentioned being told Cruise does not  
  use braille because “people do not use braille much anymore.”  

  Commissioner Revi met with the Dallas Bicycle Coalition and will continue to do so to  
  ensure people with disabilities are included in bicycling and bicycling events.  
  Commissioner Revi discussed Lime Vehicles and the bicycles/tricycles available to  
  people with disabilities for free upon request.

            Transitioning from NCEC to Developmental Delay 

Noncategorical Early Childhood (NCEC) is defined as a student between the ages of 3-5  
who is evaluated as having an intellectual disability, an emotional disability, a specific  
learning disability, or autism but whose ARD committee describes as NCEC instead. 

                                              “NCEC” transitioning to  
                                              “Developmental Delay” 

Developmental Delay: 
     ▪  Definition listed in the rule; based on extensive public comment, the final rule will allow the use of this  
       category from ages 3 through 9 years of age. 
          ▪  No district will be required to use it (however, if a district uses it, the district must use the  
            definition and criteria listed). 
          ▪  A district MAY begin using this beginning with the 2024-2025 school year. 
          ▪  NCEC must no longer be used beginning with the 2025-2026 school year. Any eligible student who  
            begins the 2025-2026 school year with NCEC, the student can maintain this eligibility until the  
            required re-evaluation before the age of 6.
